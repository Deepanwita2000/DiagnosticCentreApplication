Tests

Blood Tests

The clinical pathology test list entails blood tests that can help assess your overall health and identify potential problems. Generally, your doctor determines which tests are right for you based on your health condition.

Imaging Tests

Pathology tests list also entail imaging tests that use different technologies to create images of certain areas of your body, which can then be used to diagnose certain conditions.

Urine Tests

Urine tests are an essential part of the pathology test list for a complete health checkup. These tests can help identify problems with your kidneys or other organs and give your doctor important information about your overall health.

Genetic Tests

A genetic test is among the critical pathology tests list used to detect a wide range of conditions, including inherited diseases, chromosomal abnormalities, and even some types of cancer.

Biopsy

A biopsy is a medical test under the pathology tests list in which tissue or cells are removed from the body for examination. Biopsies are performed for many reasons, including diagnosing cancer, infections, and other conditions.

Stool Tests

Stool testing is also a part of the pathology test list that is performed to check for various health conditions.

MRI

MRI is a type of diagnostic test that can create detailed images of nearly every structure and organ inside the body.

X-rays

X-rays are a form of electromagnetic radiation, similar to visible light. Unlike light, however, x-rays have higher energy and can pass through most objects, including the body. Medical x-rays are used to generate images of tissues and structures inside the body.
